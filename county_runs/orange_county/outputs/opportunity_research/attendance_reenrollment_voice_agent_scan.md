# Attendance And Re-Enrollment Voice Agent Scan

As of March 31, 2026, the 29-district LCAP corpus provides strong support for an `attendance / chronic absenteeism / family follow-up` pitch and much weaker support for a `competitive re-enrollment / public-vs-private leakage` pitch.

## Executive Read

### What the LCAPs strongly support

- districts are explicitly funding attendance monitoring, attendance intervention, truancy prevention, SART/SARB processes, multilingual family communication, and home-school outreach
- districts repeatedly describe overloaded manual workflows:
  - monitoring absences
  - sending letters
  - escalating to families
  - identifying barriers
  - documenting interventions
  - coordinating SART/SARB and attendance teams
- this is exactly where an outbound voice agent can fit

### What the LCAPs weakly support

- very few districts explicitly describe `re-enrollment decline`, `family leakage to private/charter/homeschool`, or `student retention risk`
- there are a few enrollment-adjacent signals:
  - `increase enrollment`
  - `marketing plan`
  - `annual re-enrollment portal`
  - `ADA`
- but the corpus does not directly document competitive loss to alternative schooling

## 1. Attendance / Chronic Absenteeism Signal

The existing opportunity pipeline already shows this as one of the cleanest commercial lanes:

- `29 / 29` districts
- `164` attendance-related actions
- `117` matched metrics in the chronic absenteeism area
- `$186.5M` in direct action dollars
- `$131.3M` in estimated externalizable dollars
- `25 / 29` districts flagged with negative pressure

This is one of the strongest “there is money + there is pain + there is operational overload” stories in the whole corpus.

## 2. Family Communication / Outreach Signal

The second major supporting lane for a voice-agent pitch is family engagement / outreach:

- `29 / 29` districts
- `283` family/community engagement actions
- `132` matched metrics
- `$645.6M` in direct action dollars
- `$511.4M` in estimated externalizable dollars

Not all of that money is clean communication spend, but it shows that districts are consistently budgeting for parent outreach, multilingual engagement, liaison work, and community-facing communication systems.

## 5. What The LCAPs Say About Re-Enrollment / Enrollment / Retention

This is the weak side of the corpus.

### Explicit re-enrollment

Only one direct re-enrollment reference was found:

- **Cypress School District**
  - `Communication` — `$156,347`
  - text includes:
    - `Utilize the parent portal for annual re-enrollment`
    - emails, phone calls, text messages
    - two-way family communication

That is useful, but it is not a broad countywide pattern.

### Explicit increase-enrollment / marketing language

The clearest direct examples are:

- **Westminster School District**
  - `Marketing Plan: Recruitment and Retention` — `$300,000`
  - `Marketing Plan: Increase Enrollment` — `$296,850`
- **Orange Unified School District**
  - `Increase Attendance and Enrollment Rates` — `$365,156`
- **Placentia Yorba Linda Unified School District**
  - `Innovative Programs` — `$207,068`
  - action text says it is intended to `increase enrollment and student engagement`

### General enrollment references

There are broader `enrollment` mentions in `22` districts, but many of those are not district retention problems. They are things like:

- AP enrollment
- dual enrollment
- CTE enrollment
- GATE / program enrollment
- special program enrollment

Those are not the same as district-level family retention.

### Explicit homeschool / private school / charter leakage

In this corpus:

- `0` districts explicitly mention `homeschool`
- `0` districts explicitly mention `private school`
- `0` districts explicitly mention `charter`

So the LCAPs do not give you direct documentary proof for that storyline, even if it is true in practice.

## 6. What This Means For Product Positioning

### Strongest pitch from the LCAPs

The cleanest LCAP-supported pitch is:

`attendance operations automation for overworked district and site teams`

More specifically:

- first-day / same-day absence follow-up
- reason-for-absence capture
- confirmation that the student is safe / home
- multilingual family outreach
- escalation rules for chronic absence risk
- call + SMS + voicemail + documentation
- handoff into SART/SARB / attendance team workflows

This pitch is directly supported by the documents.

### Medium-strength adjacent pitch

`family communication / re-engagement infrastructure`

This is supported by:

- parent communication actions
- community liaison actions
- home visits
- multilingual engagement tools
- attendance campaigns
- parent portals

This is broader than attendance and can include enrollment-related comms.

### Weakest LCAP-supported pitch

`win back families leaving public education`

This may still be commercially real, but the LCAPs do not document it well.

If you want to sell into that problem, you will probably need non-LCAP evidence such as:

- district enrollment trends by school
- board meeting discussions of declining enrollment / closures
- transfer-out codes and exit reasons
- parent surveys
- school closure / consolidation memos
- interdistrict transfer patterns
- private / charter penetration data by geography

## 7. Best LCAP-Derived Data Fields For A Voice Agent Thesis

You can pull and use the following from the LCAPs:

- district chronic absenteeism metrics
- attendance rate and sometimes ADA metrics
- subgroup-specific chronic absenteeism rates
- school-site-specific absenteeism hotspots
- named attendance software/platforms
- named communication platforms
- presence of SART/SARB / truancy teams
- home visit and parent communication workflows
- family/community liaison staffing
- attendance incentive / re-engagement programs
- district language around barriers, re-engagement, and family support

## 8. What The LCAPs Do Not Really Give You

- clean reason-code data for why students are absent
- family-level contact rates
- call answer rates
- contact attempt volumes
- turnaround time from absence to outreach
- actual re-enrollment conversion funnels
- explicit family churn to homeschool / private / charter
- precise revenue-at-risk per student inside the document itself

## 9. Sales Read

If you want the strongest LCAP-backed version of the story, it is:

`Districts already know chronic absenteeism is hurting them. They are funding attendance systems, dashboards, liaisons, SART/SARB processes, and family outreach, but those workflows are still manual, fragmented, and labor-heavy.`

That makes an outbound voice agent credible if it is positioned as:

- reducing same-day follow-up burden
- capturing structured absence reasons
- escalating risk early
- documenting outreach automatically
- supporting multilingual family communication
- helping attendance teams spend time on the hardest cases instead of routine calls

For `re-enrollment`, the documents support a narrower version:

`family communication and enrollment support infrastructure`

They do not strongly support:

`families are fleeing to homeschool/private/charter and districts are explicitly budgeting to stop it`
