# Santa Clara County Attendance Pitch Report

As of 2026-03-31, this report uses the 28-district Santa Clara LCAP corpus and the county opportunity analysis to size chronic absenteeism demand, separate broad ecosystem spend from cleaner attendance line items, and surface the most sales-relevant district examples.

## Headline Numbers

- Broad chronic absenteeism / attendance-recovery footprint: **27 / 28 districts**, **170 actions**, **51 metrics**, and **$139,816,311** in matched action dollars.
- Estimated externalizable share of that broad footprint: **$114,569,381**.
- Metric pressure: **19 districts** show negative pressure, with **51.3%** of movement points worsening and **46.2%** of pace-scorable metrics off track or moving away.
- Conservative lower bound of explicit attendance-titled spend: **$3,780,493** across **41** distinct actions in **19** districts.
- Even narrower workflow/communications lower bound: **$294,360** across **12** actions in **7** districts.
- Embedded but not separately priced: **12** explicit attendance actions in **7** districts carry **$0 standalone budget**, which usually means the work is embedded inside larger staffing or student-services lines.

## What The Money Means

There are two valid numbers in this market, and they answer different questions:

1. **Broad market sizing:** the county is already directing about **$139,816,311** toward the chronic absenteeism problem set. This is the right number for showing that attendance is budget-real, county-wide, and operationally important.
2. **Clean attendance-ops budget:** the lower-bound of clearly named attendance actions is only about **$3,780,493**. This is the right number for showing what districts are willing to call out explicitly as attendance work.

The gap between those numbers is the commercial insight. A lot of attendance work is **buried inside student services, counseling, liaison, climate, wellness, and admin staffing**, which means the operational need is real even when there is not a neat attendance software line item.

## Pitch Takeaways

- Chronic absenteeism is county-wide: **27 of 28** districts are explicitly in the problem set, and **19** show negative metric pressure.
- The broad attendance ecosystem is large enough to matter, but the clean line items are much smaller. That means you should sell against **buried labor and embedded workflow pain**, not just ask for a new software budget.
- The cleanest product wedge is not generic AI calling. It is **attendance operations infrastructure**: same-day family outreach, multilingual follow-up, barrier capture, re-engagement workflows, escalation to attendance teams, and audit-ready notes back into district systems.
- Districts already describe the exact manual workflows your product would touch: **family conferences, personal outreach, attendance messaging, A2A-style monitoring, SART/SARB processes, home visits, attendance teams, child welfare and attendance staffing, and community liaisons**.
- The strongest near-term prospects are districts where **explicit attendance actions exist and negative pressure remains**. In this corpus that includes Santa Clara Unified, Evergreen, Gilroy, Fremont Union, Los Altos, Mt. Pleasant, and San José; Palo Alto is still attractive because the workflow is unusually explicit even without the same level of metric pressure.
